Arnhem Postal History

Welcome to the Arnhem Postal History Project, a collection of stamps, covers, correspondence, photographs and documents from the Netherlands during World War II. Through the lens of postal services – including postmarks, censor markings, and routing – the collection offers a glimpse of everyday life in tumultuous times. The project includes a database of images, people and places represented by the objects in the collection; exhibits providing more in depth views of time periods and events; and short essays focused items of interest.

Exhibits

The exhibits are expanded versions of exhibits entered into competition at multiple philatelic shows. The first two completed are The Evolution of the Holocaust in Holland and False Hopes and Lasting Thanks: The Battle of Arnhem, with exhibits in development on Code Name Yellow: 1940-Invasion and Occupation of Holland, Daily Life in Occupied Holland, and The Hunger Winter and Liberation.

False Hopes and Lasting Thanks: The Battle of Arnhem

Operation Market Garden, in September of 1944, raised hopes of liberation and the end of four years of German occupation. This exhibit looks at the Battle of Arnhem and its aftermath from the perspectives of the Dutch civilians and German and British combatants whose lives intersected for ten days in Arnhem.

learn more

Code Name Yellow: 1940-Invasion and Occupation of Holland

This exhibit and collection will follow the Netherlands during the period of mobilization, especially following the German invasion of Poland in September of 1939, and its experience of the German invasion. German troops crossed the border in the pre-dawn hours of Friday, May 10th, 1940 and by September 15th controlled the country except for a small area of Zeeland, soon to fall.
